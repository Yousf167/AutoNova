Hennessey Performance Engineering®



Hennessey Performance Engineering (HPE)® is an American hypercar manufacturer and high-performance vehicle creator.

In addition to building the Venom F5 hypercar, the company specializes in 'making fast cars faster' modifying sports cars from several brands including Chevrolet, Dodge, Cadillac, Jeep, Ford, GMC, and Lincoln. Established in 1991 by John Hennessey, their main facility is located 45 minutes west of Houston, Texas in Sealy, Texas. The firm focuses on mechanical component modification for creating high-powered cars. Besides performance automobiles, they also tune pickup trucks and sport utility vehicles such as the Ford Raptor, the Ram TRX and the Jeep Grand Cherokee. Hennessey Venom GT

In 2010, Hennessey Performance introduced the Hennessey Venom GT, a high-performance sports car that made headlines for its remarkable speed. Here are the key details:

  • Base Model:
    • Based on the Lotus Exige platform.
    • Equipped with a twin-turbocharged V8 engine.
    • Engine power: 1,244 horsepower (928 kW).
    • Weight: 1,244 kilograms (2,743 pounds).
    • Top speed: 270.49 mph (435.31 km/h).
  • Special Editions:
    • World's Fastest Edition: Three coupés produced in 2014 to celebrate the car's speed record.
    • Venom GT Spyder: A roadster variant requested by Aerosmith lead singer Steven Tyler.
      • Based on the Lotus Elise.
      • Added 30 pounds (14 kilograms) due to structural changes.
      • Limited production: Five units.
      • One-off “Final edition” model, 6 units in total.
      • Final edition gained 204 hp (207 PS; 152 kW) and was 9 pounds (4 kilograms) lighter than the regular Spyder1.

The Hennessey Venom GT series has a history of breaking speed records and continues to captivate automotive enthusiasts worldwide.

Hennessey Venom F5


The Hennessey Venom F5 is indeed a marvel of engineering, boasting impressive specifications that set it apart as a hypercar designed to push the boundaries of speed. Here's a summary of its key features:

  • Acceleration: 0-60 mph (0-97 km/h) in 2.0 seconds
  • Top Speed: 301 mph (484 km/h)
  • Engine: Bespoke 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V8
  • Power: 1,817 hp (1,355 kW; 1,842 PS)
  • Torque: 1,193 lb⋅ft (1,617 N⋅m)
  • Performance Predictions:
    • 0-186 mph (0-300 km/h) in under 10 seconds
    • 0-249 mph (0-400 km/h) in under 20 seconds
  • production: Limited to 24 units
  • Price: US$1.6 million

The partnership with Pennzoil and Shell aims to achieve the ambitious 300 mph top speed goal. With only 24 units being produced, the Venom F5 is set to be an exclusive addition to the world of hypercars.

Hennessey Exorcist

The Hennessey Exorcist package transforms the Chevrolet Camaro ZL1 into a powerhouse with a suite of performance upgrades. Key highlights include:

  • Engine: Customized with a high-flow supercharger, intercooler, and a bespoke valvetrain, achieving 1,000 hp and 1,310 N⋅m of torque.
  • Performance: Accelerates from 0-60 mph in 3.5 seconds, completes a quarter-mile in under 10 seconds, and has a top speed of 217 mph.
  • Transmition: Automatic versions require a transmission upgrade costing US$9,950.
  • Optional Packages:
    • Drag Pack: Enhancements for drag racing, priced at US$8,995.
    • Road Race Pack: Lightweight wheels and high-performance tires, priced at US$6,995.
    • Cost: The upgrade adds US$57,455 to the base price of the ZL1.

Owners can order the Exorcist package directly or send their ZL1 to Hennessey for conversion. The result is a significantly more powerful and faster vehicle than the standard ZL1.
