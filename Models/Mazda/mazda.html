About Mazda

Mazda Motor Corporation is a Japanese multinational automaker based in Hiroshima, Japan.

Founded in 1920, Mazda initially started as a cork manufacturing company. It later transitioned into producing machine tools before venturing into automobiles in the 1930s.

Mazda is known for its innovative engineering, including the development of the rotary engine, which powered several iconic models such as the RX-7 and RX-8.

Today, Mazda produces a range of vehicles known for their stylish design, fuel efficiency, and driving dynamics, appealing to a global customer base.
